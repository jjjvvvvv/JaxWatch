# JaxWatch BD Strategy

## Data Source Tiers

### Tier 1 (Primary) — DIA
- DIA Board
- DIA committees
- DIA term sheets
- DIA resolutions
- DIA RFPs / RFQs

**Treatment:** Deepest parsing, money extraction, importance weighting, narrative summaries.

### Tier 2 (Secondary) — Design Review
- DDRB
- Planning Commission

**Treatment:** Confidence boosters, timeline indicators, friction signals.

### Tier 3 (Contextual) — Formal Approvals
- City Council
- JTA (when relevant)
- Other agencies

**Treatment:** Track for completeness, don't prioritize.

---

## Project Lifecycle

A real downtown project follows this arc:

1. **DIA** = Project birth
   - Vision alignment
   - Developer chosen
   - Public incentives structured

2. **DDRB** = Project adolescence
   - Design review
   - Neighborhood friction
   - Iterations

3. **Council** = Project adulthood
   - Formal approvals
   - Rubber stamp or political theater

---

## What Makes a Real Lead

A good lead is NOT: "A developer name appears in a document"

A good lead IS: "A project that is politically visible, time-sensitive, under-coordinated, and mid-process."

### Typical profiles:
- Out-of-town developers unfamiliar with Jacksonville
- Public-private partnerships
- First-time downtown builders
- Projects touching DIA + DDRB + Council
- Projects involving public money but unclear execution

### High-signal DIA artifacts:
- **Term sheets** — real money, real developers
- **RFPs / RFQs / developer selection**
- **Board actions approving incentives**
- **Land disposition agreements**
- **Master development agreements**
- **Extensions / amendments** — often signal trouble or confusion

---

## Where NOT to Spend Time

- Routine permits
- Small variances
- Single-parcel residential
- Administrative approvals
- One-off signage or façade changes

These don't have budget, don't need consultants, don't scale.

---

## Signal Types by Source

### DIA (hunt here)
Projects entering a complexity phase. Not cold leads.

### Planning Commission (early awareness)
Large rezonings, PUDs, unusual deferrals, repeated continuances.
Track and wait — don't pitch yet.

### DDRB (intervention timing)
- 2+ continuations
- Staff recommending denial
- Neighborhood opposition
- Major design revisions requested

Moments when developers are frustrated and outside help becomes attractive.
